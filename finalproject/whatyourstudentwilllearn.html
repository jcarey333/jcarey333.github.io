What Your Student Should Know

  1. Match letters to sounds: Be able to identify the sounds associated with letters.


  2. Recognize letters: Know the alphabet and the difference between uppercase and lowercase letters



  3. Read grade-level books: Read aloud and understand books at their reading level.


  4. Write letters and words: Write and share information in a variety of ways, including drawing, writing letters and words, and speaking out loud.



  5. Comprehend stories: Ask and answer questions about stories, and talk about the characters, settings, and major events.



  6. Learn new words: Learn and use new words to express thoughts, feelings, and ideas.



  7. Understand basic grammar: Know basic grammar skills, like capitalization and punctuation.



  8. Do basic math: Do addition and subtraction problems with numbers 0 to 20.


  9. Understand direction: Know right from left.


  10. Understand maps: Locate the United States, the seven continents, and the four oceans on a map or globe.

What Your Student Will Learn

  1. Reading: Read aloud and understand first grade-level books, and read at least 150 high-frequency words. They should also be able to recognize the difference between letters and words, and understand that print represents spoken language.





  2. Math: Count, read, write, and sequence numbers up to 100. They should also be able to add and subtract small numbers, and compare numbers using greater than, less than, and equal symbols.




  3. Science: Understand that all matter has observable properties, and that energy can change form. They should also be able to recognize patterns in weather, and understand how living things interact with their environment.




  4. Geography: Name and locate the seven continents on a world map, and identify basic directions on a map using a compass rose. They should also be able to say what state and city they live in, and share facts about different cultures.




  5. Writing: Try to write and spell new words phonetically.



  6. Other: Know right from left, and work independently for short periods of time.
